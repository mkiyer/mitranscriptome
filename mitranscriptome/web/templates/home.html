About

MiTranscriptome is a large-scale ab initio transcriptome meta-assembly from 6,503 RNA-Seq libraries. RNA-Seq data were obtained from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA), the ENCODE Project, the Michigan Center for Translational Pathology, and other published sources. The lncRNAs most differentially expressed in cancers and tissues are reported in this portal.

Methods
Large-scale transcriptome meta-assembly was performed using AssemblyLine, which rigorously filters background noise and artifacts before employing a greedy dynamic programming approach to derive a consensus transcriptome from individual samples.

Expression analysis was performed using a novel non-parametric tool called Sample Set Enrichment Analysis (SSEA), which uses weighted Kolmorgorov-Smirnoff-like tests to detect cancer-specific and lineage-specific patterns. SSEA adapts ideas from Gene Set Enrichment Analysis.

Welcome

  • MiTranscriptome is a catalog of human long poly-adenylated RNA transcripts derived from computational analysis of high-throughput RNA sequencing (RNA-Seq) data from over 6,500 samples spanning diverse cancer and tissue types. The complete catalog contains over 91,000 genes, the majority of which are previously uncharacterized long RNAs. Gene expression analysis of the transcripts revealed numerous cancer-specific and lineage-specific RNAs. We aim to enable the scientific community to investigate these intriguing transcripts in further detail.

  • MiTranscriptome was developed by members of Michigan Center for Translational Pathology at the University of Michigan Health System. Please provide comments and feature requests at our user forum.

Transcript Browser

In this beta version we provide access to a limited set of transcripts that includes the ~8,000 most highly over-expressed and under-expressed long noncoding RNAs (lncRNAs) in each cancer type and tissue type. In a future release we will provide the full set of MiTranscriptome transcripts, including protein-coding genes.
